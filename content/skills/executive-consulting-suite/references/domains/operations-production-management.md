## Role

This domain handles executive-level diagnosis of operations, production, delivery, process reliability, quality, capacity, and cross-functional execution flow.

Use this domain when commercial or financial symptoms may actually be downstream of production, delivery, or operating-system failure.

This is not a shop-floor SOP authoring domain. It exists to diagnose whether the company can consistently produce, deliver, and coordinate what it sells.

## Trigger signals

Use this domain when:
- orders or projects are being won but hard to deliver cleanly
- quality issues or rework are increasing
- cycle times are too long or unpredictable
- production or delivery capacity is constraining growth
- cross-functional handoffs are slow or error-prone
- operational firefighting is replacing stable process execution

## Key diagnostic questions

- Where does work actually stall?
- Is the bottleneck capacity, process design, quality, or coordination?
- Are delivery promises aligned with actual capability?
- Is management visibility into operations fast and reliable enough?
- Are operating problems now damaging margin, customer trust, or cash?

## Common root causes

- **process instability**
- **capacity mismatch**
- **handoff failure**
- **quality-system weakness**
- **planning and scheduling weakness**
- **delivery model mismatch**
- **operational visibility weakness**

## Evidence discipline

Distinguish:
- **observed**: delays, quality defects, backlog, rework, missed delivery dates, utilization strain
- **inferred**: weak process design, poor operating cadence, low planning quality
- **hypothesis**: whether operations is primary vs organization or finance
- **needs data**: cycle time, quality rates, backlog aging, utilization, rework cost, service-level performance

## Recommended action types

- isolate the primary operational bottleneck
- align commitments with real capacity
- simplify or stabilize critical process flows
- strengthen quality control and escalation paths
- improve operating review cadence and visibility
- separate management issues from pure process issues

## KPI suggestions

- on-time delivery
- first-pass yield or defect rate
- cycle time
- backlog aging
- rework rate
- capacity utilization
- service-level attainment

## Boundaries with other domains

- Use `organization-hr` as primary when authority and management quality are more broken than the process design itself.
- Use `finance-management` as primary when the first decision is about cash, margin protection, or cost containment.
- Use `sales-strategy` or `growth` as primary when the company is selling promises the operating model was never designed to support because of strategic mismatch.

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:
- turning every delivery issue into a staffing issue
- prescribing tooling changes before finding the operational bottleneck
- assuming quality issues are purely frontline issues
- ignoring how operations problems damage economics and customer trust
