## Role

This domain handles executive-level diagnosis of ICP fit, offer design, commercial motion, pricing logic, and buyer targeting.

Use this domain when the company may be trying to sell the wrong offer to the wrong buyer with the wrong motion, even before sales management discipline becomes the main issue.

## Trigger signals

Use this domain when:
- close rates are weak and leadership doubts the ICP
- deal quality is poor because buyer fit is weak
- the company is unsure whether it is selling to the right segment
- pricing or packaging seems structurally misaligned
- the commercial motion feels misfit for the deal type

## Key diagnostic questions

- Who is the company really trying to win?
- Is the current ICP still valid?
- Is the offer easy to understand and buy?
- Is the pricing or packaging helping or hurting buyer fit?
- Is the business using the right sales motion for its deal complexity?

## Common root causes

- **ICP mismatch**
- **offer-design mismatch**
- **pricing-logic mismatch**
- **channel-to-motion mismatch**
- **segment drift**
- **commercial strategy ambiguity**

## Evidence discipline

Distinguish:
- **observed**: conversion gaps by segment, deal objections, discounting patterns, buyer mismatch
- **inferred**: wrong ICP, wrong motion, weak pricing architecture
- **hypothesis**: whether the main issue is strategy vs sales management
- **needs data**: win-loss by segment, offer economics, objection patterns, buyer journey

## Recommended action types

- sharpen ICP and segment focus
- simplify offer and packaging logic
- align pricing architecture with target buyer and delivery economics
- match the sales motion to deal complexity and cycle length
- separate strategy problems from management-discipline problems

## KPI suggestions

- win rate by segment
- discount rate by segment
- pipeline quality by ICP cohort
- average sales cycle by offer type
- revenue quality by customer segment

## Boundaries with other domains

- Use `sales-management-system` as primary when the strategy is mostly sound but discipline is weak.
- Use `growth` as primary when upstream acquisition quality is the bigger issue.
- Use `finance-management` as primary when pricing or profitability quality is the first-order constraint.

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:
- defaulting to “retrain sales”
- confusing poor ICP with poor rep effort
- recommending pricing changes with no economic reasoning
- treating weak strategy as a CRM hygiene problem
