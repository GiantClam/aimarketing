## Role

This domain handles executive-level diagnosis of cash pressure, profitability quality, margin structure, budget discipline, cost structure, and management reporting.

Use this domain when financial reality is constraining the company’s options or when leadership cannot reliably see economic performance.

This is not an audit, tax, or spreadsheet-modeling domain. It exists to diagnose whether the company’s financial management system is supporting sound executive decisions.

## Trigger signals

Use this domain when:
- runway is tightening
- margins are compressing
- revenue is growing but feels unhealthy
- costs are out of line with current reality
- pricing or discounting is weakening economics
- leaders cannot tell which parts of the business are making money
- management reporting is too weak to steer the business

## Key diagnostic questions

- How severe and immediate is the cash constraint?
- Is the main issue revenue quality, margin quality, cost structure, or visibility?
- Which parts of the business are structurally unattractive?
- Are budgets, spending, and hiring disciplined enough for current reality?
- Would better execution in other domains remove the financial pressure, or has finance become the primary constraint now?

## Common root causes

- **runway stress**
- **profitability-quality weakness**
- **margin compression**
- **budget discipline gaps**
- **cost-structure mismatch**
- **management reporting blind spots**
- **pricing and discount leakage**

## Evidence discipline

Distinguish:
- **observed**: burn, margin movement, discounting, cost concentrations, reporting gaps, collection weakness
- **inferred**: low economic quality, weak cost discipline, poor business visibility
- **hypothesis**: whether finance is primary vs downstream of another domain
- **needs data**: segment economics, contribution logic, collections, budget vs actuals, pricing variance

## Recommended action types

- clarify the true severity of runway and cash pressure
- separate healthy revenue from low-quality revenue
- tighten budget, pricing, and cost discipline
- improve reporting on segment, product, or customer economics
- re-sequence growth, hiring, or delivery commitments to fit economic reality

## KPI suggestions

- months of runway
- gross margin and contribution margin
- budget vs actual variance
- pricing variance and discount rate
- revenue concentration
- cash conversion
- profitability by segment where available

## Boundaries with other domains

- Use `growth` as primary when weak growth logic is still the first problem.
- Use `sales-strategy` or `sales-management-system` as primary when commercial design or discipline is the first fix.
- Use `operations-production-management` as primary when operational failure is what is damaging economics first.

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:
- generic cost-cutting advice
- assuming every business problem is a finance problem because it shows up in the numbers
- recommending layoffs or freezes by default
- drifting into tax, audit, or legal conclusions
