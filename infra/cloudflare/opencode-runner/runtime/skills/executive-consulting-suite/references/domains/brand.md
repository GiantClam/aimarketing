## Role

This domain handles executive-level diagnosis of brand positioning, category clarity, market trust, and external narrative fit.

Use this domain when the business appears to have a market-interpretation problem: the right people do not understand, trust, or value the company the way leadership expects.

This is not a design or content-production domain. It exists to diagnose whether the brand system is helping the business become understood and chosen.

## Trigger signals

Use this domain when signals like these appear:
- the company feels hard to describe clearly
- the market knows the company but does not trust or prefer it enough
- the brand promise feels inconsistent across channels or teams
- awareness exists, but buyer confidence remains weak
- leadership suspects a positioning or category-definition problem

## Key diagnostic questions

- What does the company want to be known for?
- Does the market describe the company the same way?
- Is the problem lack of awareness, lack of trust, or lack of clarity?
- Has a recent repositioning weakened how the market interprets the business?
- Is weak demand partly caused by a weak or confused external narrative?

## Common root causes

- **positioning ambiguity**
- **category confusion**
- **trust deficit**
- **promise inconsistency**
- **brand-message fragmentation**
- **brand-story drift after strategic or product change**

## Evidence discipline

Distinguish:
- **observed**: market feedback, inbound quality, conversion behavior, customer language
- **inferred**: market confusion, trust weakness, promise mismatch
- **hypothesis**: whether brand is primary rather than growth or sales
- **needs data**: segment-level message response, customer interviews, comparative win-loss inputs

Do not say “the brand is weak” unless you can tie that to a business outcome.

## Recommended action types

- clarify category and positioning
- simplify the core external promise
- align leadership and GTM language around one buyer-facing narrative
- separate trust-building problems from acquisition problems
- test whether brand confusion is upstream of growth and sales symptoms

## KPI suggestions

- aided and unaided recall where relevant
- buyer message comprehension
- branded demand quality
- trust or credibility objections in the sales process
- win-loss language patterns tied to market understanding

## Boundaries with other domains

- Use `growth` as primary when the main issue is channel and demand-generation logic.
- Use `sales-strategy` as primary when the main issue is ICP, offer, or motion rather than market interpretation.
- Use `brand` as primary when the company is being misunderstood before the demand engine even has a chance to work.

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:
- vague “品牌要做强” advice
- visual-brand recommendations with no business logic
- treating awareness as automatically positive
- confusing market-understanding problems with channel problems
