## Role

This domain handles executive-level diagnosis of organizational structure, authority, management quality, accountability, role clarity, and incentive alignment.

Use this domain when the visible business problem is materially driven by how the company is organized, led, managed, staffed, or incentivized.

This is not a policy-drafting or HR-operations domain. It exists to diagnose whether the people and management system can translate strategy into coordinated execution.

## Trigger signals

Use this domain when:
- the team is busy but execution remains slow
- leadership is not aligned
- decisions are repeatedly revisited
- authority and ownership are unclear
- manager quality varies sharply
- hiring outpaced management capacity
- incentives reward the wrong behavior

## Key diagnostic questions

- Are leaders aligned on priorities and tradeoffs?
- Are decision rights explicit?
- Are managers creating standards and accountability?
- Are key outcomes clearly owned?
- Are performance, incentives, and authority aligned?
- Is founder dependency creating structural bottlenecks?

## Common root causes

- **leadership misalignment**
- **authority confusion**
- **role ambiguity**
- **manager quality weakness**
- **incentive mismatch**
- **founder bottleneck**
- **staffing design breakdown**

## Evidence discipline

Distinguish:
- **observed**: decision churn, duplicated work, ownership gaps, turnover, uneven management quality
- **inferred**: misalignment, weak authority design, incentive distortion
- **hypothesis**: whether organization is the primary bottleneck
- **needs data**: org design detail, span-of-control patterns, attrition patterns, manager variance

## Recommended action types

- clarify decision rights and ownership
- narrow executive priorities
- redesign critical role scopes and reporting interfaces
- strengthen manager expectations and inspection
- align incentives with company-level outcomes
- reduce founder bottleneck and authority concentration

## KPI suggestions

- decision cycle time
- accountability clarity for critical outcomes
- manager-to-team performance variance
- regretted attrition
- time-to-productivity for key roles
- meeting and approval load

## Boundaries with other domains

- Use `sales-management-system` as primary when the issue is mostly commercial discipline.
- Use `operations-production-management` as primary when the main issue is process and delivery stability.
- Use `finance-management` as primary when cost, margin, or cash is the first-order constraint.

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:
- vague culture advice
- generic communication prescriptions
- defaulting to reorgs before diagnosing authority and management quality
- recommending headcount moves without checking financial and management capacity
