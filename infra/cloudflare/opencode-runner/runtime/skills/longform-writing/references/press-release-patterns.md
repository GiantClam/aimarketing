# Press Release Patterns

Use this reference only when writing a press release or other news-style announcement.

## Standard Structure

### 1. Headline

- State the news clearly.
- Prefer concrete nouns and verbs over hype.
- Keep it easy to scan and quote.

### 2. Optional Subheadline

- Add useful context, scope, or significance.
- Do not repeat the headline in softer words.

### 3. Dateline

Typical pattern:

`CITY, State/Country – Month Day, Year`

Only include location if the format or user context calls for it.

### 4. Lead Paragraph

Answer the key announcement questions immediately:

- What happened
- Who is involved
- Why it matters
- When relevant, when or where it is happening

### 5. Supporting Body

Use the next paragraphs to add:

- Important supporting facts
- Practical context
- Product or partnership details
- Rollout or timing information

Prioritize information in descending order of importance.

### 6. Quote

- Attribute quotes to a real, approved spokesperson.
- Keep the quote human and quotable.
- Use it to add interpretation, commitment, or significance, not to restate the lead word-for-word.

### 7. Boilerplate

Short company background:

- What the company is
- Who it serves
- What it offers

Keep this stable and reusable.

### 8. Media Contact

Include only when requested or when the press release is clearly intended for external distribution.

## Common Scenarios

### Product Launch

- Lead with the launch
- Clarify who it is for
- State what problem it solves
- Include availability or rollout timing if approved

### Partnership Announcement

- Identify both parties
- State the purpose of the partnership
- Explain the practical significance

### Funding Announcement

- State the funding event
- Include amount and round only if approved
- Explain what the company will do next with the funding

### Event Or Milestone Announcement

- State the milestone or event clearly
- Explain why it matters now
- Add next-step or attendance context if relevant

## Avoid

- Generic praise with no facts
- Fabricated quotes
- Overloaded headline puns
- Burying the announcement under backstory
- Blog-style opinion paragraphs before the news
