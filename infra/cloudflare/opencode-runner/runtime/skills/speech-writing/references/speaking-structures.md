# Speaking Structures

Use this reference when the request is for a speech, remarks, or host script and you need a reliable spoken structure.

## Speech Opening Patterns

- Start with a clear observation the audience immediately understands
- Start with a short story beat that creates tension
- Start with a contrast: what people assume versus what is actually true
- Start with a direct statement of why this moment matters

Avoid slow background exposition before the audience has a reason to care.

## Body Organization Patterns

### Three-Beat Structure

Useful for short speeches and keynotes:

1. Frame the problem or opportunity
2. Develop the insight or argument
3. Land the implication or call forward

### Journey Structure

Useful for founder stories or reflective talks:

1. Where we started
2. What changed
3. What we learned
4. What comes next

### Task-Oriented Spoken Explanation

Useful for spoken presentations that still need clarity:

1. What matters
2. Why it matters
3. What to do next

## Closing Patterns

- Return to the opening idea with new meaning
- End on a clear forward-looking line
- End on a call to act, reflect, or commit
- End on a short memorable sentence, not a summary paragraph

## Short Remarks Templates

### Opening Remarks

- Welcome the audience
- Name the occasion
- State why the gathering matters
- Transition into the event or next speaker

### Closing Remarks

- Acknowledge the event or effort
- Thank key people
- Leave the audience with one clear closing thought

### Thank-You Remarks

- Name the contribution
- Express appreciation plainly
- Close with respect and brevity

## Host Script Transition Patterns

- Move from one segment to the next by naming what just happened and what comes next
- Keep guest introductions short, accurate, and energizing
- Use transitions that sound live, not robotic

Good transition pattern:

`Now that we have heard X, let's turn to Y.`

Good guest intro pattern:

`Please welcome [Name], [Role], who will help us explore [Topic].`

## Time-Based Compression Guidance

### Around 1 minute

- One message
- One opening beat
- One closing beat

### Around 3 minutes

- One clear message
- Two or three supporting beats
- Tight close

### Around 5 minutes

- One central message
- Short opening
- Three developed beats
- Strong close

### Around 10 minutes or more

- One central message
- More developed narrative or examples
- Clear sectional flow
- Distinct callback or closing image
